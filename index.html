About

This experiment investigates which liquid (milk, water, mineral sparkling water, or watermelon juice) creates the softest dough. The hypothesis is that doughs with different liquids will have different densities, with lower density indicating softer dough. The experiment involves preparing four doughs, each using a different liquid, and measuring their density after fermentation. Additional factors like texture, elasticity, and appearance are also observed. The data collected includes mass, initial and final volumes, dough volume, and density. By comparing the densities and other characteristics, the experiment aims to determine which liquid produces the softest dough. It also encourages discussion on the possible reasons behind the observed differences. Lastly, safety precautions and cleanup procedures are emphasized.
